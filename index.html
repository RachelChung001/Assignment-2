Dennis Ritchie is an American computer scientist, who created the C Programming language and is the Co-inventor of the Unix operating system.

History

Year Event
Sept 9 1941 Ritchie was born
1967 Ritchie earned a job at bell labs, where his father had worked before him. Him and a man named Kenneth Tompson began to work on a project called Unix.
1968 Ritchie graduated from Harvard with degrees in Mathematics and Physics.
1969 Bell labs removes Multics operating system which Ritchie and Tompson was working on.
1971 Ritchie and Thompson release the UNIX operating system. The UNIX was a multiuser operating system that was able to function on a variety of platforms and ultimately changed technology forever.
1973 Ritchie created a language that simplified the UNIX system, as well as make it more accessible to other computers. Thus the "C" Language was created and was implemented into the UNIX system.
1990 Ritchie is promoted to leader of the Computing Techniques Research Department at Lucent Technologies (previously known as Bell Labs). Here he managed the growth of operating systems and oversaw applications.
1996 Ritchie developed The Inferno Operating System on behalf of Bell Labs. I was a system that was a distributed operating system as free software.
1999 Ritchie and Thompson got recognized by the US president, Bill Clinton, for their work on the UNIX operating system as well as their work on the "C" programming language.
Oct 12 2011 Death of Dennis Ritchie (Cancer and heart disease)
